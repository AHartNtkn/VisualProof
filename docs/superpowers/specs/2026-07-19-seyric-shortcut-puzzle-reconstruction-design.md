# Seyric Shortcut-Puzzle Reconstruction Design

## Outcome

Reconstruct the Seyric puzzles whose empty-cut truth witness lets the player erase
the content that supposedly distinguishes the puzzle. Preserve each puzzle's point,
not its incumbent topology. A reconstructed puzzle may change dramatically, but it
must remain an open problem, remain distinct from every other puzzle, and make its
assigned structural idea genuinely relevant.

`echoed-veil` is explicitly outside reconstruction. Its puzzle, witness, guidance,
catalog treatment, coverage, progression position, and available solutions remain
unchanged. A separate puzzle will provide the elementary deiteration introduction
that `echoed-veil` does not provide well.

## Controlling Principles

- Alternative solutions are welcome. The catalog does not prescribe a unique route.
- A solution is defective for content-design purposes when it bypasses the only
  interaction that gives the puzzle its stated point and thereby collapses several
  different puzzles into the same generic exercise.
- Existing graph shape, size, witness sequence, and visual silhouette have no
  preservation claim. The puzzle ID, artifact identity, and assigned point survive.
- Canonical uniqueness is necessary but insufficient. Renaming proposition owners,
  adding irrelevant structure, or rearranging an existing decision does not create a
  new puzzle.
- Puzzle count remains emergent. This work adds one needed deiteration introduction;
  it neither targets nor preserves a quota.
- No proof rule, proof physics, engine interaction, or completion semantics changes.

## Selected Reconstruction Strategy

Use targeted first-principles reconstruction.

Literal empty-cut deletion is permitted only where the remaining statement is valid
and worthwhile. Focused semantic evaluation established that this applies only to
`shallow-edit-legality-contrast`. In every other reconstructed puzzle, the empty cut
is the load-bearing reason the statement is true, so deleting it alone would produce
an invalid goal. Those statements must be rebuilt around their assigned point.

Local patches that merely replace the empty cut with another automatic truth witness
are prohibited. Each replacement starts by defining the player-facing decision and
then constructs the smallest valid closed statement that presents that decision.

## Protected and Intentional Shortcut Puzzles

### `forked-veil`

Keep unchanged as the simple early example in which a sufficient branch makes a
sibling disposable.

### `echoed-veil`

Keep every authored surface unchanged. It remains an early open structural puzzle
and may remain an informal exposure to deiteration. It is not the collection's sole
or controlled elementary deiteration introduction.

### `atomic-fragment-erasure`

Rebuild as the deliberately intimidating optional counterpart to `forked-veil`.
The retained point is whole-fragment erasure: a visually dense, semantically complete
branch is irrelevant because a small sufficient branch already settles the theorem.
The complexity must be coherent rather than random padding, and the erasable unit
must be a compound semantic fragment rather than a renamed copy of either empty veil
in `forked-veil`.

Its catalog position and prerequisites may move later so that the intimidating
presentation is optional practice after whole-fragment selection is familiar.

## Elementary Deiteration Introduction

Add one new Seyric puzzle after `single-mark-return` and before later puzzles rely on
deiteration instruction.

The start contains one ownership ring and two exact marked veil fragments:

- the supporting marked veil occurs in an ancestor field;
- the matching marked veil occurs inside a descendant field;
- neither branch is independently sufficient;
- the descendant occurrence is removable by ordinary deiteration;
- no empty-cut truth witness exists at the start.

A focused kernel experiment established the four-step witness:

1. deiterate the descendant marked veil using the ancestor occurrence;
2. erase the now-disposable supporting fragment;
3. eliminate the now-vacuous ownership ring;
4. eliminate the exposed double cut.

The candidate has no canonical duplicate in the current catalog. Its guidance should
explain exact structural equality, ancestor support, and descendant removal in one
short passive page. It must not suggest that visual similarity across different
owners is sufficient.

## Reconstructed Puzzle Briefs

Each brief defines the point that survives. The final graph may otherwise be replaced
completely.

### `shallow-edit-legality-contrast`

Remove the empty cut directly. Retain the shallow comparison between similar targets
whose actual host polarity changes which edit is legal. Rebuild the witness against
the remaining valid statement and ensure both compared hosts contribute to the
problem rather than one being decorative.

### `atomic-content-insertion`

Present a negative field that lacks one atomic fragment required to create an exact
structural relationship used downstream. Inserting that atom must enable a meaningful
subsequent simplification. No independent contradiction or sufficient branch may
settle the theorem before the insertion decision.

### `atomic-double-cut-selection`

Require the player to choose one exact atomic occurrence for double-cut introduction.
The created annulus or changed polarity must enable the next useful operation. Nearby
content must make selecting a larger region or a different atom a materially different
choice, not merely an illegal click.

### `compound-copy-authority-contrast`

Present a coherent compound source, a legal descendant destination, and a
similar-looking destination without the same ancestor authority. Iterating the
compound into the legal destination must establish content needed later. The source,
legal target, and decoy must all participate in the visible decision; none may sit
beside an already-sufficient false branch.

### `compound-double-cut-selection`

Require double-cut introduction around an intact compound proposition. Selecting an
internal atom or a visually adjacent larger fragment must fail to create the needed
workspace. The compound's internal grouping must matter after the introduction so
the puzzle is not the atomic selection problem with extra marks.

### `content-bearing-annulus-choice`

Present an eligible content-bearing double cut and an obstructed near-match. Removing
the eligible pair must preserve content that is subsequently needed; acting on the
near-match must be refused or lead to a visibly different state. An empty annulus is
allowed as a double-cut side condition, but no empty cut may independently prove the
whole statement.

### `double-cut-insertion-workspace`

Make double-cut introduction create a temporary workspace whose annulus has the
polarity needed for a useful insertion. The inserted content must then participate in
an exact match, transfer, or discharge. This must differ from
`atomic-double-cut-selection`: the primary decision is why a workspace is needed,
not which atom to wrap.

### `grouped-branch-construction`

Require construction of a compound branch as one semantic unit. The group must feed
a genuine alternative or downstream consumer, and its internal conjunction must be
preserved. It must not reduce to atomic insertion with two adjacent atoms, nor may an
existing sufficient branch let the player discard the constructed branch.

### `polarity-bubble-contrast`

Use ownership rings and cut parity to place similar marked fragments in hosts with
different effective edit permissions. The player must identify the actual host, not
infer legality from local visual depth or color. Both sides must be necessary to the
resulting proof, and the statement must not be settled by removing one whole side.

### `useful-vacuous-owner-workspace`

Make a vacuous ownership ring useful as a temporary structural workspace. Introducing
it must enable a later exact open-pattern or ownership-sensitive operation that is not
available beforehand. The puzzle must differ from ordinary double-cut workspace
creation in both boundary type and downstream use.

## Content and Progression Migration

For every reconstructed puzzle, update the authoritative bundle together:

- puzzle diagram;
- replaying solution witness;
- coverage assignment, visible situation, and defeated misconception;
- passive guidance, when the assigned point is introduced there;
- catalog function text where the old topology is described;
- prerequisites and folio order where the new decision depends on earlier concepts.

Existing puzzle IDs and artifact identities remain stable. Saved attempts whose
logical fingerprints no longer match are already rejected by the existing save
validation contract; no compatibility adapter is introduced.

The new deiteration puzzle receives a new ID and artifact entry. Automatic per-culture
numbering absorbs the added record.

## Uniqueness and Adversarial Review

Every new or reconstructed start must pass two different checks.

### Canonical uniqueness

Use the existing canonical form to prove that no two starts are isomorphic. This
rejects exact structural duplicates despite renamed IDs.

### Experiential uniqueness

Review each puzzle against the full catalog and record:

- the initial structural situation visible to the player;
- the meaningful first decision or interaction;
- what a tempting neighboring strategy gets wrong;
- why the puzzle is not an existing problem with renamed owners, larger operands, or
  rearranged decoration.

Two puzzles fail this review when the same recognition and action solve both and the
additional structure creates no new decision. Passing canonicalization does not
override that failure.

## Shortcut Audit

Add a direct structural validation for the identified defect: a negative host may not
contain an empty cut that already falsifies the host conjunction while competing
content supplies the supposed puzzle complexity.

The audit explicitly permits only:

- `forked-veil`;
- unchanged `echoed-veil`;
- the deliberately reconstructed intimidating `atomic-fragment-erasure`.

The whitelist is semantic content policy, not a generic engine exception.

The audit supplements rather than replaces replay validation. It does not attempt to
forbid all alternative proofs or require one authored move sequence.

## Validation

Completion requires:

- every authored witness replaying to blank through the existing kernel;
- every puzzle goal remaining closed;
- canonical start forms unique across the complete catalog;
- experiential uniqueness notes complete and defensible for every reconstruction;
- the shortcut audit reporting only the three explicit exceptions;
- `echoed-veil` content, validation, guidance, catalog, coverage, and progression
  projections unchanged;
- the new deiteration puzzle ordered after `single-mark-return` and before dependent
  instruction;
- catalog, coverage, guidance, progression, and validation schemas agreeing on every
  ID;
- focused content tests, full non-physics tests, TypeScript checking, content
  validation, semantic asset validation, and the renderer build passing;
- the dedicated unchanged-physics battery remaining unrun;
- the resulting work committed with an empty worktree.

## Out of Scope

- proof-rule or interaction changes;
- proof-physics changes;
- changes to `echoed-veil`;
- redesign of unaffected puzzles;
- enforcing a puzzle count or replacing one puzzle merely to preserve a count;
- forbidding legitimate alternative solutions;
- packaging or launching Electron.
