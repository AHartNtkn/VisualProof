# Opening Cultures and Content Design

**Status:** Approved
**Date:** 2026-07-11

## Outcome

Cursebreaker opens with the oldest pure-propositional sealing culture and then
reaches the proposition-binder culture. Cultural identity, artifact naming,
provenance, and direct passive guidance make the logical progression legible
without turning worldbuilding or teacher copy into puzzle-selection authority.

This design governs culture presentation and opening-content voice. Puzzle
inventory, obligation coverage, placement, progression gates, and validation are
owned by the current first-principles Seyric design and the strict game-content
format. This document defines no record count, curriculum role, or puzzle ID.

## Cultures are the puzzle genres

Each puzzle genre is diegetically a culture's sealing tradition. Cultural age,
lineage, contact, and isolation explain why its artifacts use particular
representations or have unusual mechanical habits. The opening culture is the
oldest surviving and most structurally primitive tradition, making its simple
seals a natural starting point.

A catalog culture record owns:

- stable internal identity;
- relative historical layer;
- lineage and isolation facts where known;
- curator-facing description; and
- characteristic sealing vocabulary.

These are catalog and worldbuilding facts, not puzzle-retention evidence or
runtime gates. The later visual naming and materials pass must demonstrate
coherent cultural options before production aesthetics are selected.

The opening culture records are:

- **The Seyric Horizon** — the earliest secure sealing horizon, known from
  funerary closures, threshold wards, and stone stoppers. Its self-name is
  unknown; “Seyric” is the modern archaeological exonym. Its seals use veils,
  fields, echoes, rings, and marks.
- **The Myratic Tradition** — the isolated Myrat complex, whose deliberate
  hollows may stand for complete seal-patterns. No direct Seyric lineage is
  established. Supplying a pattern is its first visible technique, not the
  identity of the whole tradition.

The names are fictional archaeological exonyms built from a shared ancient
philological stratum. **Seyric** is distantly shaped by Greek *sēma* (mark or
sign); **Myratic** is distantly shaped by Greek *martys* (witness). This is
background construction logic, not etymology shown to the player. Neither name
identifies or borrows the name of a living people.

Later revision of these culture identities requires playtest or worldbuilding
evidence, not local synonym preference.

## Progression boundary

Culture order, culture unlock requirements, gateways, folio order, puzzle
prerequisites, and the resulting progression graph belong only to
`content/progression/core.json`. Catalog lore and guidance cannot change those
facts.

The mandatory Seyric path is exactly the transitive set of Seyric completions
needed to unlock the first Myratic puzzle. Every other Seyric puzzle is practice.
No separate classification field duplicates that graph, and difficulty, teacher
copy, artifact naming, or pedagogical labels cannot change it.

The Myratic gateway demonstrates only that tradition's first visible technique.
The full culture may later develop compound and outer-dependent instantiation,
comprehension abstraction, vacuous and nested binders, ownership and shadowing,
quantifier movement and distribution, exhaustive proposition choice, and
impredicative proposition encodings. Those later topics do not authorize records
in the pure-propositional Seyric collection.

## Artifact naming and provenance

Player-facing artifact names use a professional register grounded in function,
culture, location, maker, owner, discoverer, excavation, or catalog history.
Optional curator shorthand may describe visible seal morphology. Important
gateways and discoveries may receive memorable professional names; routine
objects may use plainer excavation or accession names. Formal theorem names do
not become display titles merely because they are convenient mathematical
labels.

Every integrated puzzle has one finished artifact entry containing a
professional name and concise provenance summary and function. Curator shorthand,
accession, findspot, and attribution are optional when evidence warrants them.
Catalog prose cannot justify a puzzle whose starting situation adds no approved
logical or experiential distinction.

## Passive guidance

Guidance may provide opening instruction, completion commentary, or commentary
for an exact recognized-unwinnable state. It uses one or more single-paragraph
passive pages while control remains with the player. Optional Next actions turn
pages without changing proof state.

No puzzle requires an intentional mistake, a prescribed solution order where
alternatives are legal, a mandatory timeline detour, or completion of teacher
pages. Invalid-move thoughts are generated from authoritative move refusals by
the interaction layer; they are not authored puzzle content. An exact-state
intervention may explain recovery through the timeline only when its build-time
demonstration proves that the declared state is legally reachable.

Guidance can explain what is already visible in a worthwhile start. It cannot
generate a puzzle, define its value, select its required solution, or gate
progression.

## Content and validation authority

The strict version-3 layered package keeps semantic puzzle cores, progression,
one build-only coverage file per culture, catalog, guidance, and validation
sidecars separate. Content
validation rejects malformed semantic diagrams, missing or cyclic dependencies,
unreachable content, incomplete cross-layer bundles, build-only witnesses that
do not reach canonical blank, invalid artifact authority, and unreachable
recognized states.

Every Seyric start has one ordinary outer goal cut, an optional uninterrupted
prefix of arity-zero bubbles used only to close global proposition names, and an
atom-and-cut matrix containing no further bubbles or wires. Seyric witnesses do
not perform nontrivial binder placement, scope, ownership, introduction, or
movement; those mechanics are Myratic content. This authoring boundary does not
limit player moves.

Logical fingerprints contain canonical puzzle semantics and transitively
referenced logical-definition semantics only. Culture metadata, artifact names,
provenance, guidance, coverage, validation traces, and presentation cannot
invalidate a logical attempt.

## Scope boundaries

- No unapproved visual culture direction, character voice, or lore is invented.
- Direct interface instruction uses the actual production vocabulary.
- No existential puzzle appears in the pure-propositional Seyric culture.
- No first-order wire, equality, lambda, or later representation appears early
  merely to satisfy an instructional sequence.
- No game-only proof interaction is content-authored.
- Physics and physics validation are outside this content design.
